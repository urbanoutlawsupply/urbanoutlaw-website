Made, Not Taught

The story behind U.O.S.

My nickname is DusTa. That came from knuckle duster. Make of that what you will.

I grew up in Portsmouth. Left school with nothing worth keeping on paper. Spent my 20s the way a lot of people do — painting walls, factory floors, drink, weed, getting through the week. No plan. No direction.

I'd been drawing since I was a kid. Nobody taught me. Nobody suggested it. Realism portraiture — the kind where you spend forty hours on a face and when you hand it over the reaction is what counts. I've sold work across the UK, Australia and the United States. Never advertised once.

Then my mid 30s arrived and I ended up in London. In rooms most people only read about. Around men whose names meant something on the street in a way that had nothing to do with fame and everything to do with fear. I was knighted into a firm that traces back to the Kray twins. I came out the other side still standing, with a very precise understanding of what loyalty actually costs.

Urban Outlaw Supply Co. wasn't born from a business plan or a market gap. The brain needed an obsession so it built a brand. Handcrafted kraft notebooks. Laser cut. Precise edges. Every measurement exact. That last part matters when you have OCD — everything in its place, clean lines, nothing out of order.

U.O.S. lives inside DusTa now. Because that's where it always came from. Same compulsion. Different material.

The discipline stays. The material changes.
